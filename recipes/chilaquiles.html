Chilaquiles

Savoury, spicy and made with lots of love

Chilaquiles can be red, green, with mole, there are many variations of chilaquiles that exist. These tasty, delicious green salsa chilaquiles are a staple in the Mexican household.

Ingredients

  • 12 Corn Tortillas
  • 1 Package of Queso Fresco
  • 1/4 Cup of White Onion, Minced
  • 1lb of Tomatillos, peeled and washed
  • 1 Serrano Pepper
  • 1/2 Cup of Cillantro, finely chopped
  • 1 Teaspoon of salt
  • 1/8 Teaspoon of Black Pepper
  • 1/2 Cup of vegetable oil
  • 1/2 Cup of Mexican Sour Cream

Steps

  1. Cut the tortillas into 8 pieces each, and set aside
  2. Crumble the Queso Fresco and mix with 1/4 cup of minced White Onion
  3. Green Salsa: In a medium sized pan with water, boil the tomatillos and the Serrano Pepper at medium temperature for 5-6 minutes or untill the tomatillos look soft. Remove from the stove and set aside 1/2 cup of water in which they were boiled for later
  4. With a spoon transfer the tomatillos and the Serrano Pepper into a blender. Add the onion, garlic, cilantro, salt and pepper, and blend for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Preheat 1/2 tablespoon of oil in a small pan for 2 minutes on medium heat. Add the tomatillo salsa. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook for 5 minutes. If the salsa gets too thick, add the water that you kept. Maintain the salsa hot until you use it.
  6. Fry the tortillas: In a deep pot, heat up the remaining oil for 4 to 5 minutes on medium-high heat, or until the oil gets to 375°F. Fry the tortillas in batches for 2 to 3 minutes, stirring constantly. Remove from the oil and let sit on a paper towel.
  7. To serve: Place a portion on a plate, add 1/3 cup of green salsa, 2 tablespoons of Mexican sour cream, and cilantro. Enjoy!
