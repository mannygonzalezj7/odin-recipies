Quesadilla De Flor De Calabaza

Best quesadilla in the whole entire world.

Quesadilla De Flor De Calabaza, or Squash Blossom Quesadilla in English, is a staple in Mexican cusine. With fresh, delicious Squash Blossoms from your local farmers market, you can make an unforgettable Quesadilla.

Ingredients

  • 12 Squash Blossoms
  • 1 Tablespoon of Vegetable Oil
  • 1/4 Cup of Onion, Minced
  • 1 Garlic Clove, Minced
  • 1/2 Tablespoon of Salt.
  • 1 Serrano Pepper, Minced (optional)
  • 2 leaves of Epazote, Minced
  • 6 medium-sized Corn Tortillas.
  • 3/4 Cup of Queso Fresco, Sliced or Grated
  • Cliced Cilantro
  • Salsa (optional)
s

Steps

  1. Wash your hands with soap and water
  2. Cut the stem of every flower and wash delicately with cold water. Place the flowers on a towel to dry the excess water. Pick the petals off or cut the flower in pieces.
  3. Pre-heat oil in a small pan on medium heat. Add the onion and salt. Stir and cook for approximately 3 minutes. Add garlic and pepper, if desired. Mix and cook for 2 or 3 minutes. Add the flowers and stir delicately until they wither, approximately 1 to 2 minutes. Remove from the heat and mix in epazote if desired.
  4. Pre-heat a skillet at medium heat. Place a tortilla in the skillet and heat up both sides for approximately 30 seconds, or until there is air pockets in the tortillas.
  5. Crumble 2 tablespoons of cheese on half a tortilla. Add 2 tablespoons of the flower into the mix.
  6. Use a spatula to fold the empty side over the flower side. Press softly with the spatula. Cook until the cheese has melted and the tortilla starts to get crispy. Flip on the other side so it gets crispy if you want. Take off the heat and continue making the rest of the quesadillas.
  7. To serve, cut each quesadilla in half ot inn triangles and serve with minced cilantro and salsa, if desired, or add other options that you would like. Enjoy!
