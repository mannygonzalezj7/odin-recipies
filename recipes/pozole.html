Pozole Rojo

Rich red stew, a family staple.

Mexican red pozole is a rich broth with corn, mild pork, and a flavorful sauce made with pasilla ancho chile and guajillo chile. Customize each dish with add-ins like sliced radishes, fresh cilantro, chopped onion, and lime.

Ingredients

For the Pozole

  • 3 pounds boneless pork shoulder, cut into inch-thick pieces
  • 64 ounces of chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons of whole Mexican oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of Cilantro leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black ground pepper
  • 3 cans of hominy for pozole (maiz pozolero), drained and rinsed

For the Salsa de Chile

  • 2 Pasilla Ancho Chili Pods, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 Guajillo Whole Chili Pods, stemmed and seeded
  • 1 cup of boiling water
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1 white onion, peeled and cut into fourths
  • 3 cloves of fresh garlic
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cumin, ground
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt

Steps

  1. For the Pozole, place the pork and broth in a 5-quart pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, skimming to remove any foam from the surface. Add the bay leaves, oregano, cilantro, salt and pepper. Reduce heat to medium-low; and cover the pot. Cook over low heat for 1 and a half hours or until the meat is tender.
  2. Meanwhile, for the sauce, place the chiles in a large heatproof bowl. Add boiling water; and then cover it. Soak the chiles for 30 minutes or until they soften. Remove the chiles with tongs or a slotted spoon and transfer them to the blender container, discarding the liquid from them. Add remaining 1/2 cup water, onion, garlic, cloves, cumin, and salt; and cover the blender. Blend on high speed until well blended. Reserve.
  3. Transfer the pork to a large bowl, reserving the cooking liquid in the pot. Cut the pork into large pieces with 2 forks. Return the pork to the pot. Add the hominy and the prepared chili sauce; and put to boil. Reduce heat to medium-low; cook over low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Serve the pozole in deep plates. Add sliced radishes, fresh cilantro, chopped onion, and lemon, if desired.
